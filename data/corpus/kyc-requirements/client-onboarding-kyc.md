# Client Onboarding KYC Requirements

## Overview

Know Your Client (KYC) procedures must be completed before any investment recommendation is made or any transaction is executed on a new account. Incomplete KYC is grounds for account suspension and may result in regulatory action against the advisor and the firm.

## Identity Verification

The client's identity must be verified using government-issued photo identification. Acceptable documents include a valid passport, driver's license, or national identity card. For corporate accounts, articles of incorporation, certificate of good standing, and identification of all beneficial owners holding 25% or more must be obtained.

## Financial Profile Collection

The following financial information must be collected and documented: annual income from all sources, total net worth excluding primary residence, liquid net worth (assets readily convertible to cash), current investment portfolio composition and approximate values, fixed financial obligations including mortgage, loans, and alimony, and expected major expenditures in the next five years.

## Investment Profile Assessment

The investment profile must capture the client's primary investment objective (capital preservation, income, balanced growth, aggressive growth), risk tolerance assessed through a standardized questionnaire, investment time horizon for the account, investment experience by asset class (equities, fixed income, derivatives, alternatives), and any investment restrictions or preferences (ethical, religious, sector-based).

## Enhanced Due Diligence Triggers

Enhanced due diligence is required when the client is a politically exposed person (PEP) or an immediate family member of a PEP, the client resides in a jurisdiction identified as high-risk for money laundering, the source of funds cannot be readily explained by the client's stated occupation and income, the account opening involves unusual circumstances such as third-party funding or urgent processing requests, or the client has adverse media coverage related to financial crimes.

## Ongoing Monitoring

KYC information must be reviewed and updated at least annually for active accounts. Material changes in the client's circumstances (employment change, inheritance, divorce, retirement) require an immediate KYC update and a review of all outstanding recommendations for continued suitability.

## Document Retention

All KYC documentation must be retained for seven years following the closure of the account or the termination of the advisory relationship, whichever is later.
