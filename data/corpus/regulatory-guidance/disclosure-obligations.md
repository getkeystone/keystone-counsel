# Disclosure Obligations for Financial Advisors

## Overview

Financial advisors must provide clear, accurate, and timely disclosures to clients regarding fees, conflicts of interest, material risks, and the nature of the advisory relationship. Disclosure is not optional and cannot be waived by the client.

## Fee Disclosure Requirements

All fees must be disclosed in writing before the advisory relationship begins. This includes management fees expressed as both a percentage and a dollar amount, performance-based fee structures and their calculation methodology, transaction costs including commissions and spreads, custody and account maintenance fees, and any fee-sharing arrangements with third parties. Fee changes require 60 days written notice to the client.

## Conflict of Interest Disclosure

Advisors must disclose any material conflict of interest that could reasonably be expected to influence the advice provided. This includes proprietary product recommendations where the advisor's firm earns manufacturing revenue, referral arrangements with compensation, personal trading in the same securities recommended to clients, and any outside business activities that could create a conflict. Conflicts must be disclosed at the time of the recommendation, not after the transaction.

## Material Risk Disclosure

Before recommending any investment, the advisor must disclose the principal risks specific to that investment, including market risk, credit risk, liquidity risk, concentration risk, and currency risk where applicable. Generic risk warnings are insufficient. The disclosure must be specific to the product and the client's circumstances.

## Relationship Disclosure

At the start of the advisory relationship, the advisor must provide a written relationship disclosure document that explains the type of relationship (advisory vs. transactional), the services that will be provided, the standard of care that applies (suitability vs. fiduciary), how complaints can be filed, and the compensation structure.

## Record Retention

All disclosure documents must be retained for a minimum of seven years from the date of the last transaction in the account. Electronic retention is acceptable provided the records are readily accessible and reproducible.
