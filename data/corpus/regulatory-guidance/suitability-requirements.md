# Suitability Requirements for Investment Recommendations

## Overview

All investment recommendations must be suitable for the specific client based on their financial situation, investment objectives, risk tolerance, and time horizon. This requirement applies regardless of the product type or transaction size.

## Know Your Client (KYC) Obligations

Before making any recommendation, the advisor must have current and complete KYC information including:

- Client's annual income and net worth
- Investment objectives (growth, income, preservation)
- Risk tolerance (conservative, moderate, aggressive)
- Time horizon for the investment
- Existing portfolio composition
- Any relevant personal circumstances (upcoming retirement, dependents, health issues)

## Suitability Assessment Standard

A recommendation is suitable when it is consistent with the client's stated objectives and risk tolerance, appropriate given their financial capacity to absorb potential losses, and not inconsistent with any known constraints or restrictions on the account.

## Documentation Requirements

Every suitability assessment must be documented at the time of the recommendation, retained for a minimum of seven years, and available for regulatory review upon request. The documentation must include the rationale for why the recommendation is suitable for this specific client.

## Escalation Triggers

A recommendation requires supervisor review when it involves concentrated positions exceeding 25% of the portfolio, leveraged or complex products, transactions that appear inconsistent with stated objectives, or clients who have been flagged for vulnerability indicators.
