# Risk Tolerance Assessment Methodology

## Overview

Risk tolerance assessment is a required component of the suitability determination process. The methodology must produce a documented, repeatable, and defensible classification of the client's willingness and capacity to accept investment risk. Subjective assessments without structured methodology are insufficient for regulatory compliance.

## Two-Dimensional Assessment

Risk tolerance has two independent dimensions that must be assessed separately. Risk willingness is the client's psychological comfort with investment volatility and potential loss. Risk capacity is the client's financial ability to absorb losses without material impact on their standard of living or financial goals. The lower of the two dimensions determines the applicable risk classification.

## Risk Willingness Assessment

Risk willingness is assessed through a standardized questionnaire of at least 10 questions covering the client's reaction to hypothetical portfolio losses of 10%, 20%, and 30%, their investment time horizon and willingness to wait for recovery, their past behavior during market downturns, their preference between certainty and potential upside, and their understanding of the relationship between risk and return. The questionnaire must be administered directly to the client, not completed by the advisor on the client's behalf.

## Risk Capacity Assessment

Risk capacity is a quantitative assessment based on the client's financial profile. Key inputs include the ratio of liquid assets to annual fixed obligations (a ratio below 3:1 indicates limited capacity), the proportion of total net worth represented by the investment account, dependence on the investment portfolio for current income, employment stability and income predictability, and insurance coverage for catastrophic events. A client with high willingness but low capacity must be classified at the lower capacity level.

## Classification Scale

The assessment produces a five-point classification: Conservative (preservation of capital, minimal volatility tolerance), Moderately Conservative (income-focused with limited growth, low volatility tolerance), Moderate (balanced growth and income, medium volatility tolerance), Moderately Aggressive (growth-focused with some income, above-average volatility tolerance), and Aggressive (maximum growth, high volatility tolerance accepted). Each classification maps to a model portfolio allocation range that defines acceptable equity, fixed income, and alternative investment proportions.

## Documentation Requirements

The completed risk assessment must include the date of assessment, the questionnaire responses with the willingness score, the capacity calculation with supporting financial data, the resulting classification and rationale, any override of the calculated classification with documented justification, and the advisor's signature and the client's acknowledgment signature.

## Reassessment Triggers

A new risk assessment is required when the client experiences a material life event (marriage, divorce, death of spouse, retirement, disability), when the client's financial profile changes by more than 25% in either direction, when the client requests a change in investment strategy, at minimum every 36 months for active accounts, or when market conditions have resulted in portfolio losses exceeding the client's stated tolerance threshold.
