# Fiduciary Duty Standard: Regulatory Evolution and Firm Obligations

## Overview

This opinion addresses the current state of the fiduciary duty standard as it applies to registered investment advisors and the implications for firm compliance programs. The standard has evolved significantly since 2020 and firms must ensure their policies reflect current regulatory expectations.

## The Best Interest Standard

The best interest standard requires that advisors act in the best interest of the client at the time the recommendation is made. This is distinct from the older suitability standard in that it imposes an affirmative obligation to consider the client's interests ahead of the advisor's own economic interests. The standard applies to the recommendation itself, not merely to the disclosure of conflicts.

## Duty of Care

The duty of care requires a reasonable investigation of the investment before recommending it to a client. This includes understanding the product's risks, costs, and features, evaluating reasonable alternatives, and considering whether the recommendation is in the client's best interest given their specific profile. A recommendation that is suitable but not in the client's best interest may violate the duty of care.

## Duty of Loyalty

The duty of loyalty requires the advisor to place the client's interests ahead of their own. This does not prohibit all conflicts of interest but requires that conflicts be identified, disclosed, and mitigated. Where mitigation is insufficient, the conflict must be eliminated. Revenue-sharing arrangements, proprietary product preferences, and compensation structures that incentivize particular recommendations are areas of particular scrutiny.

## Implications for Compliance Programs

Firms must maintain written supervisory procedures that address best interest documentation at the time of each recommendation, conflict identification and mitigation protocols, reasonably available alternatives analysis, and cost comparison documentation. The compliance program must be reasonably designed to achieve compliance with the best interest standard, not merely to detect violations after they occur.

## Enforcement Trends

Recent enforcement actions have focused on three areas: inadequate alternatives analysis where advisors recommended higher-cost products without documenting why lower-cost alternatives were not suitable, undisclosed revenue-sharing that created incentives not visible to the client, and supervisory failures where firms lacked systems to monitor for best interest compliance. Penalties have included disgorgement, fines, and in severe cases, registration revocation.

## Recommendation

Firms should conduct a gap analysis of their current compliance programs against the best interest standard, with particular attention to documentation requirements and alternatives analysis procedures. This analysis should be completed within 90 days and any identified gaps remediated within 180 days.
